GUARDIAN IN BOX

A phishing email is a fraudulent email designed to trick recipients into providing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details. Cybercriminals use phishing emails to impersonate legitimate organizations and steal data or spread malware.

How to Identify a Phishing Email

  1. Suspicious Sender: Check for unusual or misspelled email addresses.
  2. Urgent Language: Look for threats or urgency like "Immediate action required!"
  3. Unusual Links/Attachments: Hover over links (without clicking) to check URLs.
  4. Poor Grammar: Typos, awkward sentences, or unprofessional formatting.
  5. Request for Personal Info: Legitimate companies never ask for sensitive info via email.
  6. Generic Greetings: "Dear Customer" instead of your name.

Stay cautious and verify before clicking!
